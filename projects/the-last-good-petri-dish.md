# The Last Good Petri Dish

*A short story — placeholder draft.*

The incubator had been beeping for an hour before anyone admitted they could hear it.

By then the power had been out across the whole east wing, and the only light in the lab came from the standby LED on the freezer — a small, patient green eye that did not care whether the samples inside it survived the night. Mara cared. Mara cared so much that she had stopped being able to think.

She opened the incubator anyway. Warm air, the smell of agar and something faintly sweet underneath it. Forty-one plates, and on forty of them, nothing. On the forty-first, a single colony, exactly where it should not have been, glowing the soft impossible blue of a thing that had decided not to follow the rules.

> "You're not supposed to be here," she told it.
>
> The colony, reasonably, said nothing.

She should have autoclaved it. Instead she labeled it, dated it, and slid it into her coat pocket like a stone from a beach you can't explain wanting.
