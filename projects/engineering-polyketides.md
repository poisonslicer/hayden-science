# Engineering polyketides

Retooling nature's molecular assembly lines to build antibiotics that don't exist yet.

## The idea

**Polyketide synthases (PKSs)** are giant modular enzymes that build macrolide antibiotics one chemical step at a time — like a factory line where each station adds a piece. Swap the stations around and, in principle, the line builds something new.

## What I did

- Swapped **rapamycin modules into the pikromycin synthase** to probe how far this engineering can be pushed
- Synthesized **100+ novel polyketides** along the way
- Led a small undergrad team to build **36 new synthases in two weeks**
- Coauthored **two papers**, including a CRISPR-Cas9 system for module-swapping the pikromycin synthase in its native host

> Most "hard" problems in the lab are really just patience problems with good controls.

## Why it matters

The antibiotic pipeline is running dry. Combinatorial biosynthesis is one of the few ways to generate genuinely new chemical scaffolds at scale.
